# Moments and MGFs

## Moments
The $n$-th moment of a random variable $X$ is defined as $E[X^n]$. 
- The 1st moment is the Mean.
- The 2nd central moment is the Variance.

## Moment Generating Functions (MGF)
The MGF is a magical function $M_X(t) = E[e^{tX}]$. If you can find the MGF, you can find any moment of the distribution by taking its derivative and evaluating it at $t=0$.


## Test Your Knowledge

<MathExample title="Example: Using the MGF for a Poisson">
  <MathQuestion>
    The Moment Generating Function of a Poisson distribution with rate $\lambda$ is $M_X(t) = e^{\lambda(e^t - 1)}$. Use the MGF to find the First Moment (the Mean) of the Poisson distribution.
  </MathQuestion>
  <MathSolution>
    To find the first moment, we take the first derivative of the MGF with respect to $t$, and evaluate it at $t=0$.
    
    $\frac{d}{dt} M_X(t) = \frac{d}{dt} e^{\lambda(e^t - 1)}$
    
    Using the chain rule:
    $M_X'(t) = e^{\lambda(e^t - 1)} \cdot \frac{d}{dt}[\lambda(e^t - 1)] = e^{\lambda(e^t - 1)} \cdot (\lambda e^t)$
    
    Evaluate at $t=0$:
    $M_X'(0) = e^{\lambda(e^0 - 1)} \cdot (\lambda e^0) = e^{\lambda(1 - 1)} \cdot (\lambda \cdot 1) = e^0 \cdot \lambda = 1 \cdot \lambda = \lambda$
    
    Thus, the Mean of the Poisson distribution is exactly **$\lambda$**.
  </MathSolution>
</MathExample>
