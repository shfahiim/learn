# Maximum Likelihood and Least Squares

Linear regression is the simplest yet most fundamental tool in machine learning for predicting a continuous target variable $y$ from input features $\mathbf{x}$.

---

## 1. The Generative Model

We assume that the target variable $y$ is generated by a linear combination of the input vector $\mathbf{x}$ plus some random noise $\epsilon$:

$$
y = \mathbf{w}^T \mathbf{x} + \epsilon
$$

Usually, we assume the noise $\epsilon$ follows a Gaussian distribution with mean $0$ and variance $\sigma^2$:
$$
\epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2)
$$

This means the conditional distribution of $y$ given $\mathbf{x}$ is:
$$
p(y \mid \mathbf{x}, \mathbf{w}, \sigma^2) = \mathcal{N}(y \mid \mathbf{w}^T \mathbf{x}, \sigma^2)
$$

---

## 2. Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE)

Given a dataset of $N$ independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) observations $\{(\mathbf{x}_n, y_n)\}$, the **Likelihood function** is the product of the probabilities of each observation:

$$
L(\mathbf{w}) = p(\mathbf{y} \mid \mathbf{X}, \mathbf{w}, \sigma^2) = \prod_{n=1}^N \mathcal{N}(y_n \mid \mathbf{w}^T \mathbf{x}_n, \sigma^2)
$$

To find the optimal $\mathbf{w}$, we maximize the **Log-Likelihood**:

$$
\ln L(\mathbf{w}) = -\frac{N}{2} \ln(2\pi\sigma^2) - \frac{1}{2\sigma^2} \sum_{n=1}^N (y_n - \mathbf{w}^T \mathbf{x}_n)^2
$$

---

## 3. Ordinary Least Squares (OLS)

Maximizing the log-likelihood above is equivalent to **minimizing the Sum of Squared Errors (SSE)**:

$$
E(\mathbf{w}) = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{n=1}^N (y_n - \mathbf{w}^T \mathbf{x}_n)^2
$$

This is why we call it "Least Squares." The solution can be found analytically using the **Normal Equations**:

$$
\mathbf{w}_{ML} = (\mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{X})^{-1} \mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{y}
$$
