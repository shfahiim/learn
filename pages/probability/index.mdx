# Probability Theory & Statistical Inference

A rigorous survey of probability theory, ranging from foundational axioms and counting principles to multivariate calculus, limit theorems, and Information Theory. This guide bridges the gap between intuitive concepts and the formal mathematics that underpin modern Data Science and Machine Learning.

## How this guide is organized

### 1. [Foundations](./probability/foundations)
The core language of uncertainty. We establish the Kolmogorov axioms, [Independence](./probability/foundations/independence), and the critical debate between [Frequentist and Bayesian interpretations](./probability/foundations/interpretations). We also introduce [Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE)](./probability/foundations/mle).

### 2. [Discrete Distributions](./probability/discrete)
Modeling "countable" events. From the simple [Bernoulli trial](./probability/discrete/bernoulli) to the [Poisson limit](./probability/discrete/poisson) of rare events.

### 3. [Continuous Distributions](./probability/continuous)
Modeling measurements. We break down the anatomy of the [Normal distribution](./probability/continuous/normal) and the memoryless nature of the [Exponential distribution](./probability/continuous/exponential).

### 4. [Multivariate Probability](./probability/multivariate)
Moving from one variable to many. We explore [Joint Distributions](./probability/multivariate/fundamentals), the "slicing" of [Conditional Distributions](./probability/multivariate/conditional), and the [Covariance Matrix](./probability/multivariate/covariance-correlation) used to map relationships in high-dimensional space.

### 5. [Mathematical Tools](./probability/mathematical-tools)
The engine room of probability. We use [Moments & MGFs](./probability/mathematical-tools/moments) to uniquely identify distributions, and the [Jacobian](./probability/mathematical-tools/jacobian) to map complex variable transformations.

### 6. [Inference Distributions](./probability/inference-distributions)
The distributions used for testing and sampling. This includes the [Chi-Square](./probability/inference-distributions/chi-square), [Student's t](./probability/inference-distributions/students-t), and the flexible [Beta/Gamma families](./probability/inference-distributions/gamma-beta).

### 7. [Limit Theorems](./probability/limit-theorems)
The "God-Tier" theorems that bring order to chaos. We prove how the [Law of Large Numbers](./probability/limit-theorems/lln) forces averages to converge, and how the [Central Limit Theorem](./probability/limit-theorems/clt) makes the Bell Curve appear everywhere.

### 8. [Information Theory](./probability/information-theory)
Probability as a measure of surprise. We define [Entropy](./probability/information-theory/entropy) and KL-Divergence, the fundamental metrics for measuring information loss and training modern AI.
