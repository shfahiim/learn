# Markov's Inequality

Markov's inequality gives an upper bound for the probability that a non-negative random variable is greater than or equal to some positive constant.

$P(X \ge a) \le \frac{E[X]}{a}$

It is the stepping stone used to prove Chebyshev's inequality!


## Test Your Knowledge

<MathExample title="Example: Using Markov's Inequality">
  <MathQuestion>
    A factory produces lightbulbs with an average lifespan of 1000 hours. The distribution is unknown, but lifespan must be non-negative. Use Markov's Inequality to find an upper bound on the probability that a lightbulb lasts *at least* 4000 hours.
  </MathQuestion>
  <MathSolution>
    Markov's formula: $P(X \ge a) \le \frac{E[X]}{a}$
    
    - $E[X] = 1000$
    - $a = 4000$
    
    $P(X \ge 4000) \le \frac{1000}{4000} = 0.25$
    
    Without knowing anything else about the distribution, we guarantee that **at most 25%** of the lightbulbs will last 4000 hours or more.
  </MathSolution>
</MathExample>
